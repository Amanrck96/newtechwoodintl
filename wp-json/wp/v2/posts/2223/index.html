Peruvian teak, or Spanish walnut add elegance and style to any outdoor construction project, from a backyard pool deck to a salt-water dock. The problem? Wood and water don\u2019t get along well, and over time, wood needs constant maintenance, repair, and replacement \u2013 and that gets expensive and time-consuming.<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

That\u2019s why homeowners, today, are turning to high-quality wood-hued composites<\/strong> that win the war against weather and water.<\/p>\n

Quality wood-toned composite looks like real wood, and feels like real wood when you walk barefoot across the new deck. And, it\u2019s eco-friendly, composed of recycled high-density plastic and wood fibers, to create a composite that stands up to weather, and whisks away water without damaging the composite.<\/p>\n

Pressure Treated (PT) Lumber<\/strong>
Pressure treated (PT) wood is, in fact, Southern pine \u2013 a softer wood that dents, scratches, and ultimately, gives in to heavy rains, snow drifts, or constant sunlight that causes this wood to twist and torque, splinter and crack \u2013 even with the best of care.<\/p>\n

PT wood is, often, the least expensive option to install, but the maintenance takes time and money over the years, and eventually, that PT lumber is going to lose the weather war. Mother Nature doesn\u2019t give up.<\/p>\n

Natural Wood<\/strong>
Natural wood is beautiful to look at, but isn\u2019t always the best choice for an outdoor build.<\/p>\n

Why would you use a natural wood that wears over time in a salt water environment? Coastal builds need durable materials to stand up to the salt-air and the sea spray that corrodes even the most durable natural woods.<\/p>\n

Exotic woods, like Brazilian ipe, can be budget busters, and finding replacement planking when you need to replace weather-worn boards can be a problem \u2013 a very expensive problem.<\/p>\n

With care, these exotic woods will stand up to weather and water better than PT lumber, but natural woods of any kind still require heavy maintenance to keep the look you want. Natural woods simply can\u2019t stand up to the elements the way wood composite can.<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

Wood-Toned Composite<\/a><\/strong><\/span>
Wood-toned composite blanking is often the best solution to exterior builds that will be exposed to rain, snow, sun, and harsh weather conditions down by the seashore or lakeside.<\/p>\n

However, there are various grades of composite from inexpensive low-end to high-quality.<\/p>\n

Low-cost wood composite has a fake-looking sheen to it. It lacks many of the features that better composites offer and, it becomes slick when used as pool decking or a beachside boat dock.<\/p>\n

Look for higher grade composite with the features to stand up to weather, water \u2013 whatever nature throws your way.<\/p>\n

Higher-grade composite should be fully clad in a strong-water-resistant casing \u2013 even the tongue and grooves. You won\u2019t see this weather-resistant cladding, but you\u2019ll enjoy its benefits for decades. Better grade composite is protected with a \u201cskin\u201d<\/a><\/strong><\/span> that resists staining from chlorinated pool water, or that harsh wear-and-tear of salt water.<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

Mold and Mildew Protection?<\/a><\/strong><\/span>
One of the biggest problems with using PT lumber, or other natural wood, is mold and mildew that thrive in damp and wet environments. It starts as a small black spot under the deck, and grows silently until you\u2019ve got an expensive repair on your hands.<\/p>\n

When you purchase composite wood products for your outdoor builds, be sure to ask about mold and mildew inhibitors. Composite wood is engineered to prevent the growth of potentially dangerous molds and mildew that can spread illness among family and friends, cause allergies to flare up, and ultimately, require your deck to be rebuilt.<\/p>\n

Composite woods also come in a variety of elegant wood hues<\/strong> that add eye appeal and unparalleled durability against water, or weather \u2013 no matter where you live.<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

\u00a0<\/p>\n

Choose from Peruvian Teak, Roman Antique, Westminster Gray, Spanish Walnut, Hawaiian Charcoal, or exotic Brazilian Ipe. Mix and match to create a personal design statement that you\u2019ll share with pride for many years to come.<\/p>\n

Wood composite<\/a><\/strong><\/span>: the solution you\u2019ve been looking for.<\/p>\n

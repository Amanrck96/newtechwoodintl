<\/p>\n

Pressure-treated wood differentiates itself from its untreated cousins with a special process that imbues it with a tougher character \u2013 one that\u2019s more resistant to physical damage and moisture.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s commonly used to build decks and boardwalks.<\/p>\n

During the\u00a0treatment process<\/a>, vacuum and pressure technology are used to force chemical preservatives into standard woods like pine, fir, and spruce. This renders it a sturdier wood that is less susceptible to fungal decay, rot, and insects.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Two of the main reasons why people use pressure-treated wood decking include:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Durability<\/strong>: It\u2019s more durable than many other types of wood and better-able to resist scratches, dents, and wear. With proper care and maintenance, it can last for several years.<\/li>\n
  • Affordability<\/strong>: Pressure-treated wood is considerably cheaper than other types of wood used for decking.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

     <\/p>\n

    Just How Costly is Pressured-Treated Wood Decking<\/h2>\n

    Even though pressure-treated wood decking is cheaper to buy and install, you may spend more money on its care and maintenance in the long run. Here\u2019s why:<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    1. Splintering, Cracking, and Warping Equals High Maintenance<\/h4>\n

    After the chemical infusion process, pressure-treated wood retains some moisture. When used for decking, the wood gets exposed to the sun and air, the moisture starts to evaporate. Shrinking and warping soon follow, and you\u2019ll have yourself an ugly deck. Some decking professionals recommend application of a sealant to prevent this.<\/p>\n

    However, staining should not be done for at least one or two months because the pressure-treated wood won\u2019t accept stain properly if it\u2019s not dried out completely. If you have to wait for that long, you will need to give your deck a thorough clean before sealing it.<\/p>\n

    These are more chores to your already busy schedule each and every year.<\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    2. Chemical Risks and Health Hazards<\/h4>\n

    If not handled properly, pressure-treated wood decking can be hazardous. When the lumber is trimmed or burned, it releases harmful toxins into the environment. It should not come into contact with your food and water, or used for gardens.<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    When\u00a0working with pressure-treated wood<\/a>, ensure that you:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Work in a well-ventilated area.<\/li>\n
    • Wear a respiratory mask, safety gloves, and goggles.<\/li>\n
    • Clean up all the sawdust.<\/li>\n
    • Thoroughly wash your clothes.<\/li>\n
    • Take a shower (not a bath).<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      And if you\u2019re disposing of this type of wood, you should treat it like a potentially hazardous material. Take it to a recycling plant or hire a professional who\u2019ll properly dispose of it. Don\u2019t bury pressure-treated wood!<\/p>\n

       <\/p>\n

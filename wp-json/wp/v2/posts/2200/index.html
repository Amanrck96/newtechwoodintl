There are many aspects that will bring the style of your deck together, but those aspects are often easier to change than the deck itself. Therefore, when choosing a color for your deck, it\u2019s important to pick something you will enjoy for many years. \u00a0 While a wood deck may be painted over but that likely won\u2019t look as nice as the original color. Superior Deck & Fence, a\u00a0deck builder in Cherry Hill<\/a>, NJ, says, \u201cA wood deck can be painted much more easily than a composite deck. It\u2019s not impossible to paint one, but there will be more work involved. The new coat might not look as clean as the original either.\u201d<\/p>\n

Composite decking is made to last; you\u2019re not supposed to need to repaint it. NewTechWood color options help you find just the right color to suit the aesthetic you are looking for.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Standard Deck Colors<\/h2>\n

<\/h2>\n

What Is the Most Popular Deck Color?<\/h3>\n

When it comes to composite decking, it\u2019s thought that the most popular colors tend to match natural wood as closely as possible. Three of our six most popular colors fit that description.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

BRAZILIAN IPE (IP)<\/h4>\n

A very popular color, the Brazilian Ipe is a brown tone that leans to the dark side, giving your installation a strong look that resembles the highly prized Ipe wood without any of the maintenance issues of using real wood. \u00a0 Ipe is valued for its strength and longevity, and our composite version lasts even longer. If you\u2019re looking to add the South American wood species\u00a0habilidade<\/i>\u00a0to your deck, with a touch of Brazillian flair.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

PERUVIAN TEAK (TK)<\/h4>\n

Peruvian Teak is one of our most popular colors. It has a medium brown tone that\u2019s meant to resemble South American Teak wood, valued for its durability and elegance. \u00a0 This color will help create a classic look. If you want that South American style but with a lighter color, this will fit your needs.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

SPANISH WALNUT (WN)<\/h4>\n

Our darkest brown tone, Spanish Walnut color provides a rich natural wood look. Much less common than other walnut variations, Spanish walnut is also higher-valued. \u00a0 If you want an expensive look without paying expensive prices, this will fit the style you are looking for. No one might know it\u2019s composite but you.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

ROMAN ANTIQUE (AT)<\/h4>\n

A very light brown-gray mix, the Roman Antique color is meant to harken to the granite-built palatial colosseums and temples of ancient Rome. If you\u2019re a fan of marble, pillars, and gold highlights, this can give you the imperial look you want. \u00a0 Pair it with Greek and Roman lighting fixtures to give you that feeling of being on Mount Olympus. Keep the wine flowing on your ancient empire\u2013themed outdoor entertaining space.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

HAWAIIAN CHARCOAL (CH)<\/h4>\n

Inspired by the volcanic rocks of Hawaii, this color is a dark gray, nearly black, tone. Modern, strong, and fierce, Hawaiian Charcoal is a great way to make a statement. \u00a0 Since this is one of our more modern styles, it\u2019s best paired with modern highlights. Chrome looks great with the dark tone of this obsidian-inspired color.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

WESTMINSTER GRAY (LG)<\/h4>\n

This is our most popular gray tone. It is a medium gray that fits almost any environment and shows off a contemporary style. \u00a0 This option is best if you want a modern look that isn\u2019t as vibrant as our Hawaiian Charcoal option. Instead of pitch-black volcanic obsidian, this color emulates rocks and stones you could may in your backyard.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Special Order Deck Colors<\/h2>\n

<\/h2>\n

What Are Some Fun Deck Colors?<\/h3>\n

These colors are a bit more unique without going too far. If you\u2019re looking to add a bit of personality to your deck, these are great options.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

AUSTRALIAN RED CEDAR (RCE)<\/h4>\n

A light-medium brown, this a great color to emulate the look of cedar with a greater length of use and much less maintenance. Like Spanish Walnut, natural Australian Red Cedar is highly sought after. \u00a0 It was\u00a0nearly brought to extinction<\/a>\u00a0when Australia was first settled. This is another great option if you\u2019re looking for a wealthy look without breaking the bank.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

ENGLISH OAK (OK)<\/h4>\n

A medium brown tone with strong yellow touches, this color provides a rustic, sturdy, and reliable look for your outdoor spaces. If you\u2019re looking for simple, country living, this is a great choice for you. \u00a0 As the name implies, English Oak originated in England. However, it was brought to the United States and continues to thrive to this day. If you live in the eastern or midwestern United States, you will have likely come across an Oak tree.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

HONDURAN MAHOGANY (MAH)<\/h4>\n

A medium-dark brown with reddish inflections, mahogany is an elegant choice for your installation. If you want a South American style that\u2019s classy, this will give you a high-class look. \u00a0 However, Honduran mahogany is mid-range mahogany when compared to other types. Cuban mahogany is much more expensive because it is harder to get. Our Honduran-inspired color will give you a classy look.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

INDONESIAN MERBAU (MB)<\/h4>\n

This dark brown tone is based on a notable Southeast Asian hardwood that makes a bold statement. If you want a dark color that fits an Asian theme, this is a great option for you.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

CALIFORNIA REDWOOD (RW)<\/h4>\n

A medium brown tone with a beautiful tinge of red, this color brings elegance into your spaces. Real California redwood is popular in the western United States because of how readily available, sturdy, and long-lasting it is. \u00a0 If you already have a redwood deck but are tired of having to maintain it, this color is a great replacement. You can keep the natural look without the work.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

ICELANDIC SMOKE WHITE (SW)<\/h4>\n

This very light white-gray was inspired by the volcanic ash and snow of the Arctic Circle and brings a light and cool aura to your deck tile space. If you want a light, modern look, this will give you what you\u2019re looking for. \u00a0 For contrast, instead of pairing light items with your dark deck, pair dark items with your light deck, or with a dark home exterior.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Give Your Deck a Psychological Boost<\/h2>\n

<\/h2>\n

The following colors are believed to have a\u00a0direct effect on the mind<\/a>. Depending on what you use your deck for, these colors can boost mood and productivity.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

IRISH GREEN (SG)<\/h4>\n

This exciting and fresh light-to-medium green tone creates a splendid spring sensation. If you want your deck to be a garden of life, this will set a solid foundation. \u00a0 Psychologically, green is a color that boosts creativity and creates a sense of calm. If you\u2019re going to use your deck to create art, green is a good color. \u00a0 You may also add a pop of yellow to boost your confidence, whether that be with your furniture, lighting, or plants.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

CARIBBEAN BLUE (SB)<\/h4>\n

Fun, exciting, and bright, this medium blue tone provides a fresh feel of lovely tropical waves splashing in the sea. This is a fun color for a deck with water views, whether that is the ocean of a lake. \u00a0 Blue is also great for focusing. If you want to spend a lot of time reading or solving puzzles, blue could help keep your mind from wandering.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

SWEDISH RED (SR)<\/h4>\n

This bright medium red tone livens your installation and creates a sense of excitement. The color red can boost your adrenaline and help with physical activities. \u00a0 If you plan on doing a lot of work out on your deck, red will keep you moving. Pair it with blue to keep you focused, or to celebrate American patriotism.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

FLORIDIAN ORANGE (OR)<\/h4>\n

Bold, bright, colorful, and fun, this color makes a big, juicy impact. It takes the adrenaline boost of red and mixes it with the confidence boost of yellow. If orange is your favorite color, you\u2019ll feel the benefits. \u00a0 Black, white, and brown look great with orange. The neutral colors help to balance out the harshness of orange. \u00a0 With so many options, it can be hard to make a decision. You have to go with whatever fits your style. Consider everything that will be on your deck and build around that.<\/p>\n

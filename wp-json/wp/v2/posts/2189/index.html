Today\u2019s home construction seamlessly improves any owner\u2019s life in a variety of ways, so if you\u2019re planning the build of a smart home, be sure to check out the latest in technology and materials.<\/p>\n

A smart house starts with smart materials.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

What\u2019s a \u201csmart\u201d house?<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Quality, newly-constructed homes are built to meet the demands of busy families, and to utilize the latest technology to handle the routine operation of a family home.
\nThese homes are:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • heated and cooled\u00a0<\/strong>using deep core thermal systems that draw energy from the earth.<\/li>\n
  • warmed<\/strong>\u00a0using radiant heat that warms mass, not air, so you and your family stay comfortable on the coldest or hottest days for less money.<\/li>\n
  • convenient,<\/strong>\u00a0with automated lighting that turns on and off as you enter and leave a room, sound that follows you around the house, remote access from any smartphone \u2013 today\u2019s smart house makes life simpler.<\/li>\n
  • green,<\/strong>\u00a0using solar and wind to power the space, to use shade trees to keep the home cooler for less, and to use siding, windows, and doors that keep the interior of your smart home comfortable no matter what the weather is like outside.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    <\/p>\n

    Initially, a smart home may cost you more for all the extra features like automated thermostats and CCTV, but over time, homeowners of smart houses earn back their investment each month with lower heating and cooling bills, no matter the climate.<\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    Wood-Toned Composite Siding: Keeping It Cozy<\/strong><\/h2>\n

    Energy conservation is essential to the efficient operation of any smart home. It doesn\u2019t make any sense to add efficient, in-floor radiant heat if that heat escapes from a drafty home.
    \nThe exterior of smart homes are often built with wood-toned composite siding to keep the cold out and the warmth in.<\/p>\n

    When considering the siding of your new build or upgrade, you have lots of options \u2013 from shake cedar to aluminum and vinyl, to lightweight wood composite constructed from high-density recycled plastic and wood fibers.<\/p>\n

    Clapboard siding is popular in many regions, and wood shingles are still used, though homeowners quickly discover just how much maintenance wood and weather require to keep a home looking clean and well-kept.<\/p>\n

    Wood requires regular staining and sealant to keep it looking good, and keeping it free from mildew and mold.<\/p>\n

    Aluminum and vinyl siding don\u2019t look real. The have a shiny patina that just doesn\u2019t look natural \u2013 even from the road. These popular siding materials are easy to clean up but they\u2019re also susceptible to dents and dings. Lower `grade aluminum or vinyl siding won\u2019t help keep out the cold, either.<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

      \n

    • weather-resistant;<\/li>\n
    • easy to install;<\/li>\n
    • low maintenance;<\/li>\n
    • fabricated from recycled materials (aka green);<\/li>\n
    • extremely durable;<\/li>\n
    • designed to allow your smart home to breathe, circulating warm or cool air throughout the house.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      <\/p>\n

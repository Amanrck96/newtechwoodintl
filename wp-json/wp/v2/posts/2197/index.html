Choosing the right wood composite for your build is Step 1 in getting started on your new or upgraded addition to your home or commercial space.<\/p>\n

So what are your choices, and which composite wood product is right for your construction needs? There are lots of options to appeal to your individual taste, and your specific needs.<\/p>\n

Hollow Core Composite<\/h3>\n

Hollow core wood composite is lighter in weight, for builds where load-bearing isn\u2019t a concern, like apartment balconies or fencing. Hollow core composite is:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • lighter in weight<\/li>\n
  • appropriate for any and all weather conditions<\/li>\n
  • easy to install<\/li>\n
  • built to last<\/li>\n
  • lower in cost than solid core composite wood<\/li>\n
  • attractive with good looks in a variety of colors and wood tones<\/li>\n
  • feels and looks like real wood \u2013 even up close<\/li>\n
  • adaptable for a variety of construction needs<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    With simple-to-install end caps, your deck, patio, walkway, fence or other exterior build will look good today, and for many years to come, with minimum maintenance, and do-it-yourself ease of construction.<\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    Solid Core Composite<\/h3>\n

    Designed for heavy duty use, solid core wood composite is ideal for stair treads, and commercial upgrades, from store and restaurant patios to exterior community spaces that experience foot traffic and a variety of weather conditions.<\/p>\n

    Solid composite, natural-looking wood is durable, eye-catching attractive, and you have a long list of options from which to choose.<\/p>\n

    Once again, solid core wood composite is simple to install. NewTechWood employs a proprietary clip technology that allows individual planks to stretch and compact without warping or splitting, creating an easy-to-install build that anyone can do. In fact, all you need is a screwdriver and an afternoon to install an average-sized deck using a lock-tight system that provides your\u00a0composite decking<\/a>\u00a0with room to \u201cbreathe.\u201d<\/p>\n

    <\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    Composite Floor Tiles<\/strong><\/a>
    \nComposite wood planks are ideal for large builds that cover a lot of ground \u2013 builds like outside decks and patios.<\/p>\n

    When you\u2019re designing for a smaller space \u2013 say an apartment balcony or home entryway \u2013 consider composite wood tiles. Why?<\/p>\n

    In smaller spaces, wood tiles create a stronger \u201cpattern\u201d than simple planks. Now, if simplicity of design is the look you want, go with the planks \u2013 even in small spaces.<\/p>\n

    However, if you want to make a different design statement, consider using composite wood tiles to create patterns that are pleasing to the eye, easy-to-install using NewTechWood\u2019s sturdy grid system, and you choose the finish that works best for your build \u2013 from simple and clean lines to beautifully designed tile layouts that make a statement while standing up to heavy traffic.<\/p>\n

    Or, maybe you\u2019d like a bit more privacy. Composite fencing offers up a range of color and design options to create the work space or privacy you need.<\/p>\n

    Much of composite wood fencing can be constructed with sturdy, matte-black posts that are style-neutral and built to last.<\/p>\n

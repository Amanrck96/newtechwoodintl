What is siding?<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Siding is the material that makes up the exterior of your home. Builders use a wood frame structure and particleboard sheath to construct a house. Exterior siding protects this particleboard from moisture damage. You could also add insulation to your siding to provide a regulated temperature for your home.\u00a0Choose a siding material that complements your decor theme, budget and surrounding climate.<\/p>\n

1. The Benefits of Exterior Siding on a Home<\/h3>\n

Replacing your exterior siding can increase your home\u2019s property value because it:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Enhances your curb appeal:<\/b>\u00a0Exterior siding is the first thing your guests see when they visit your house. It makes up the majority of your home\u2019s exterior appearance.   • Protects your home:<\/b>\u00a0Siding guards your home against harsh weather conditions and extreme temperatures. Siding protects the inside of your home from wind, rain and snow.     2. When Should I Replace My Siding?<\/h3>\n

    <\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    Replace your siding to maintain the integrity of your home. You would know if you need to replace your siding if:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • You notice warping or rotting in your siding panels:<\/b>\u00a0You should walk around your home and inspect your siding once every couple of months. Certain siding materials could rot when exposed to excess moisture. You could also see cracks in certain siding panel materials. Check your siding after a harsh storm or if you hear an object hit the side of your house. Replacing your siding maintains your home\u2019s appearance and protects the interior structure.<\/li>\n
    • The siding wasn\u2019t installed properly:<\/b>\u00a0Professional builders know the various siding materials require different methods of installation. Improper installation of your home\u2019s siding could cause significant damage.\u00a0Your siding wasn\u2019t installed properly if you see nails or screws sticking out of your siding panels. You may also see worn out or wavy siding. Poor caulking would cause uneven siding panels.<\/li>\n
    • You notice rust or discoloration:<\/b>\u00a0Water damage could cause rusty nails or screws in your siding. Certain siding materials could also rust or discolor as a result of moisture damage.<\/li>\n
    • You see blisters or bubbles in the siding panels:<\/b>\u00a0Bubbles or blisters in the siding panels point to the presence of water under your siding. Moisture could ruin the integrity of your home\u2019s sheathing.\u00a0Siding is supposed to keep moisture and harsh temperatures outside of your home. Moisture inside your home means that your exterior siding isn\u2019t doing its job. Mold and mildew could be signs of moisture infiltration in your home. Check your home\u2019s interior walls for peeling or blistering paint for water damage also.<\/li>\n
    • Moisture causes mold or mildew buildup on your siding panels:\u00a0<\/b>Fungus usually feeds off any excess moisture or wet debris on your siding. The presence of fungus doesn\u2019t always mean that you need to replace your siding. After you clean the fungus off your siding, check your panels for other signs of damage.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Fall is the best time to replace your siding. Certain siding materials could expand in the hot summer sun. The same materials could also contract in the cold temperatures of the winter. Spring\u2019s excessive rain could\u00a0delay your siding project and bring moisture into your home. Fall typically has moderate temperatures and less rain or snow. Some manufacturers drop their prices in the fall, after the peak summer season.\u00a0Replacing your siding in the fall also gets your home ready for the cold winter season.<\/p>\n

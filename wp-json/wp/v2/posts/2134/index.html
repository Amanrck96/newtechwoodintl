What do you look for in a deck?<\/p>

Style? Comfort? Durability?<\/p>

<\/p>

There are so many decks that may have the right color and texture, but lack in durability and strength.\u00a0 All that beauty could be just a fa\u00e7ade covering a weak and poor quality deck that is far from being safe.\u00a0 Your deck\u2019s safety is one feature that you should never compromise on.<\/p>

It is recommended to schedule regular professional deck inspections to make sure no safety issues would go unnoticed.\u00a0 Even though most of the deck inspectors will only inspect the joists, posts, foundation, and hardware, the inspections should go further than just the surface.\u00a0 You also need to have its structure evaluated.<\/p>

Most of these problems are caused by excessive exposure to snow, rain, or humidity.<\/p>

<\/p>

The most common factors that present the greatest risk to the safety of your deck are moisture infiltration and insect damage.\u00a0 For a deck to be considered as safe, it should be both insect-proof and waterproof.<\/p>

Moisture-Resistant Decking<\/strong><\/p>

<\/p>

The safety of a deck goes hand-in-hand with its waterproof capabilities.\u00a0 Safe decks should not allow even the slightest moisture infiltration.\u00a0 Not only does moisture ruin the appearance of wooden decks \u2013 both in color and texture \u2013 but it also accelerates the rotting of the wood which compromises on the strength of the deck.<\/p>

High-quality deck composite decks are waterproof and will prevent any moisture infiltration into the deck.\u00a0 This can be attributed to the fact they are made from recycled plastic and wood fibers.<\/p>

<\/p>

The moisture-resistant feature makes composite decking a much better alternative for outdoor decking compared to wooden decks. It\u2019s suitable for regions that are wet most part of the year.<\/p>

Insect-Resistant Decking<\/strong><\/p>

Insect damage is another great concern for deck safety, and more specifically, damage from termites, carpenter bees, and carpenter ants.\u00a0 These insects are known to establish their nests and colonies under wooden decks, and will indiscriminately bore holes and alter the deck\u2019s stability.\u00a0 As a result, your deck may weaken and collapse.<\/p>

So how do you get rid of these insects?<\/p>

<\/p>

You could try the traditional pest control mechanisms like smoking and use of insecticides.\u00a0 But they will just come back when the chemicals wear off.\u00a0 You can completely solve this problem by replacing your worn-out wooden deck with a composite deck.<\/p>

Composite decks provide a better substitute because they have outer polymer layers that protect the inner core from the damage caused by pesky insects.\u00a0 In addition, they don\u2019t have the resin found in natural wood and, hence, don\u2019t attract any insects.<\/p>

Safety First with NewTechWood Composite Decking<\/strong><\/p>

With NewTechWood composite decking, deck safety isn\u2019t just superficial.<\/p>

<\/p>

Our\u00a0UltraShield decks<\/a>\u00a0combine the proved strength of high-density polyethylene plastics with that of wood fibers, and have tough outer polymer shells that encapsulate the boards to provide a resilient layer of protection.<\/p>
